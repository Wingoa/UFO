UFO Project for Digital Humanities Fall 2022

This project was created to collect, organize, and summarize a small archive of UFO reports released by the United Kingdom government in 2013. These documents include testimonials and reports from a wide range of professional law enforcement/military personnel and civilians who had personally witnessed an unidentified object.


All source materials were collected from the UFOs section of the National Archives website. All content was available under the
Open Government Licence v3.0.

Our Process

  1. Our first step was to gather all neccessary files from the National Archives website.
  2. After downloading all files, we began processing each .pdf file. This step involved reading through each file and identifying the appropriate UFO reports.
  3. We then tranformed each .pdf report to a .docx file. This step required a great deal of manual transciptions and usage of OCR software available from Adobe.
  4. Once the team had all the .pdf reports transformed into .docx files, we were able to begin inputing the text into .xml files.
  5. Along with transfering the text into .xml, we started on our .xml mark-up proccess to identify the important information in each UFO Report.
  6. We gradually continued marking-up our .xml files while we began development on our user interface.
  7. Our xml mark-up began with us identifying what information appeared to be standard throughout each UFO Report. These elements inlcuded dates, locations, organizations, enviromental conditions, key notes, and of course UFO descriptions.
  8. At this point, we created a standardized schema that we could import into each xml file.
  9. Along with the xml work, we began creating our charts and tables based on the information that we had so far. This included our xsl style sheets that we used for the report tables and the bar graphs on the statistics page.
  10. The team completed all .xml mark-up and this is where we implimented our .xslt procedures to create our .html pages.
  11. One of our last steps was refining the .xml mark-up and the layout of our .html pages, charts, and report tables.
  12. Then some final tweaks on the user interface. This was most changing the layout of some pages and adding to the global .css file.
  13. Not long after that we had what you are looking at today!
